🔤 Pronoun Case Overview

Finnish personal pronouns change form depending on their grammatical role. Learn the key forms and when to use them.

Partitive (mua/sua) → Mä rakastan sua. I love you. (with love/like verbs)
Genitive (mun/sun) → Mun talo on keltainen. My house is yellow. (possession + täytyy)
Elatiivi (musta/susta) → Musta kahvi on hyvää. In my opinion, coffee is good. (+ tykkää)
Adessive (mulla/sulla) → Mulla on kirja. I have a book. (kenellä?)
Ablative (multa/sulta) → Sain kirjan multa. from me (keneltä?)
Allative (mulle/sulle) → Onko tää mulle? to/for me (kenelle?)
