Practice adjective cases with olla

🧠 How Predicative Sentences Work

Pattern: Subject + olla + adjective. The adjective case depends on whether the subject is countable (nominative) or uncountable (partitive). Negation does NOT change the case!

UNCOUNTABLE kahvi coffee, water, love...
→
ADJECTIVE PARTITIVE hyvää, kylmää...
COUNTABLE auto car, table, person...
→
ADJECTIVE NOMINATIVE uusi, iso, kaunis...
KEY RULE:
Negative
does NOT change the case!
Kahvi on hyvää. Coffee is good. (uncountable → partitive) Kahvi ei ole hyvää. (still partitive!)
Auto on uusi. The car is new. (countable → nominative) Auto ei ole uusi. (still nominative!)
Talot ovat isoja. The houses are big. (plural → plural partitive)

📌 Countable vs. Uncountable:

Uncountable: kahvi, vesi, rakkaus Countable: auto, talo, ihminen Plural → plural partitive Neg doesn't change case!
📖 Question words: Millaista kahvi on? (uncountable)  ·  Millainen auto on? (countable)  ·  Millaisia talot ovat? (plural)

📋 Predicative Examples

kahvi (uncountable) Kahvi on hyvää. Kahvi ei ole hyvää. Coffee is / isn't good.
vesi (uncountable) Vesi on kylmää. Vesi ei ole kylmää. Water is / isn't cold.
auto (countable) Auto on uusi. Auto ei ole uusi. The car is / isn't new.
talo (countable) Talo on iso. Talo ei ole iso. The house is / isn't big.
talot (plural) Talot ovat isoja. Talot eivät ole isoja. Houses are / aren't big.
housut (always plural) Housut ovat isot. The pants are big. (pl. nom)
