🧠 How Finnish Intonation Works

Finnish intonation is about sentence endings, not dramatic pitch changes. Grammar (not pitch!) marks questions. There are two main patterns:

TERMINAL Pitch ↘ falling at end
=
MEANING I'm done. your turn to speak
CONTINUATION Pitch →↗ flat or rising
=
MEANING I have more... still speaking
QUESTIONS:
mennään
+
-ko
=
Mennäänkö? (grammar marks it, NOT pitch!)
Mennään vaan. → pitch ↘ Let's go. (terminal)
Me oltiin viikonloppuna... → pitch → We were over the weekend... (continuation)
Mennäänkö kahville? → pitch ↘ Shall we go for coffee? (-ko = question!)

📌 Key Points:

Terminal = done speaking Continuation = more to come Questions fall like statements! -ko/-kö marks questions
📖 Question words that also mark questions (no -ko needed): mikä (what), missä (where), milloin (when), kuka (who)

📋 Intonation Pattern Examples

Joo, mennään. ↘ Terminal Yeah, let's go.
Mennäänkö? ↘ Falls too! Shall we go? (-ko = question)
Me oltiin laivalla, → Continuation We were on the boat, [and...]
se oli hauska reissu. ↘ Terminal it was a fun trip. [done]
Kuitti? ↗ Rising Receipt? (casual question, rising)
Missä se on? ↘ Falls Where is it? (question word = no -ko)
